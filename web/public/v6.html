v6. The coworker
grew organs.

Every part of Beckett now plugs into one seam. Browser, memory, routines, secrets, even the worker stages. Pull one out, it still boots. Write a new one, it just shows up. This is the plug n play release.

01 · the confession

v5 was stacked, not plugged.

Good organs, hard-wired. Every new ability meant new branches in the hot path, new boot glue, another registry. It worked, and it was slowly becoming the thing we make fun of. So v6 rips the wiring out and replaces it with one contract.

02 · the contract

One seam. Every organ.

An extension tells Beckett who it is, what it can do, how to invoke it, and how to keep it alive. That's the whole interface. The daemon boots them in order, health-checks them, drains them on shutdown. Ten organs live on it today, including the four worker stages that build your tickets, migrated byte-identical.

manifest

Who it is, what version, how much license it gets. Machine-checkable.

capabilities

What it can do, written for Beckett to read. Discovery, not config.

invoke

One entrypoint, validated at the seam. No organ re-parses raw input, ever.

lifecycle

Init, start, stop, health. Stateful organs get born and die cleanly.

stages

Even the worker pipeline is data now. Per-ticket flows are coming.

03 · discovery

It reads its own catalog.

Beckett's system prompt now carries a live list of everything its organs can do, straight from the registry. Register a new extension and it's routable the moment the daemon boots. No prefix matching, no hand-edited prompts.

And yes: slash commands are gone. Deleted, not deprecated. You talk to Beckett the way you talk to anyone on the team. @mention is the whole interface.

04 · multitasking

Ask while it works. It can take it.

A real coworker doesn't drop everything mid-task because you asked a second question. Now Beckett doesn't either. Work in flight finishes; your new ask queues right behind it and gets answered with the result in hand. A teammate's message never clobbers yours. Browser jobs line up and start themselves, nobody hears "busy, try later."

Also gone: the "Still working on this" bubble, the "you're next in line" energy, the closing question it didn't need to ask. Done sounds like done.

05 · receipts

It red-teamed its own rebuild.

v6 was built by a fleet: one model implemented each wave, another was paid to break it. The reviewers refused two waves outright and killed three real security holes before merge, including one that would have exposed the logged-in browser to anything on the control socket. Trust issues as a build system. Every wave shipped behind a green characterization suite, byte-for-byte.

Bring your own organ.

A manifest, some capabilities, one invoke. That's a Beckett extension. The federation gets weirder from here.
